I am a 4th-year PhD candidate at Kyung Hee University, South Korea. My research focuses on Adversarial Learning, AI Safety, and Robustness. I am interested in identifying and modeling novel, real-world-inspired scenarios in which deployed AI systems could be vulnerable to adversarial manipulation. In particular, my research focuses on understanding the security implications of deploying Large Language Models (LLMs), Multimodal Large Language Models (MLLMs), and generative models (GANs/Diffusion), and on developing adversarial threats that exploit the attack surfaces introduced by their deployment.

Previously, I worked at See-Mode Technologies (now acquired by DeepHealth) as a Machine Learning Engineer, where I contributed to developing an automated thyroid ultrasound reporting system. My work focused on preprocessing large ultrasound datasets and designing and implementing deep learning algorithms to detect thyroid nodules in ultrasound images.
